Yudansha

Black Belts of Polish Aikido Organization

Yudansha (有段者) is a Japanese term for individuals who hold a dan rank in martial arts. Below we present all black belt holders who received their promotions within the Polish Aikido Organization, honoring their dedication and commitment to learning Aikido according to the tradition of Shihan Fumio Toyoda.

The list is ordered from the highest master grade (5th dan) to the first grade (1st dan), in accordance with Japanese dojo tradition. Within each grade, names are arranged alphabetically.

About Dan Grades

Dan grades (段) represent the level of mastery in Aikido. In Japanese tradition, yudansha signifies not only technical skills but also deep understanding of the art's philosophy, commitment to continuous improvement, and readiness to pass knowledge to others.

Each dan grade requires years of dedicated practice, passing examinations before a master commission, and meeting rigorous technical and personal requirements. Detailed examination requirements are available on the Dan Grades page.
